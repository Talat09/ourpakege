Looking at your accounts will help you identify where you’re spending. Annamaria Lusardi, the Denit Trust chair of economics and accountancy at the George Washington University School of Business, recommends getting a sense for your monthly cash flow — what’s coming in and what’s going out. 2. Categorize your expenses Start grouping your expenses. Some credit cards automatically tag your purchases in categories like department store or automotive.
